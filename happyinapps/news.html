More than 80 clinical trials launch to test of the coronavirus .

Ministry of Health 03 Feb 2019
05 Comments 33K Views

The unwise use of antibiotics has led to the emergence of bacteria that are resistant to antibiotics. This incident, known as antimicrobial resistance (AMR), has made it increasingly difficult to treat and care for patients. Director General of Health Services of the Indonesian Ministry of Health (Kemenkes) dr. Azhar Jaya, SH, SKM, MARS revealed data on antimicrobial resistance incidents reported by sentinel hospitals. The data includes two types of antibiotic-resistant bacteria.

"AMR data in Indonesia is specifically obtained from data reported by sentinel hospitals designated by the Director General of Health Services, where the results of Extended-spectrum Beta-Lactamase (ESBL) measurements in 2022 at 20 sentinel site hospitals were 68%," said Azhar in Jakarta, written on Tuesday (17/9). These two bacteria can cause death and attack all organ systems in the human body.

Impact of AMR on Patients From hospital reports received by the Ministry of Health, handling patients with antimicrobial resistance infections requires great effort. This is because bacteria that are resistant to antibiotics affect patient care. Treating patients with AMR infections is very difficult due to several factors. The first is limited drug options. Effective drugs for AMR patients may not be available or expensive and pathogens can become resistant to existing antibiotics.

WHO Global Antimicrobial Resistance and Use Surveillance System (GLASS) data updated in 2022 states that antimicrobial resistance in Escherichia coli and Klebsiella pneumoniae in Indonesia was detected through examination of blood and urine specimens of patients infected with AMR.

Second, the diagnosis is slow. Culture and sensitivity tests are needed to diagnose patients with old infections, which takes time, thus slowing down proper treatment. Then, the hospital leadership's commitment to optimizing laboratory functions is needed The third factor is related to side effects. Treatment of antimicrobial resistance often requires antibiotics with severe side effects or the risk of toxicity.

Fourth, the spread of AMR infections. Antimicrobial resistance infections can spread rapidly, especially in hospital environments, requiring strict infection control measures. Fifth, high cost. Because AMR treatment takes a long time (Length of Stay/Los is long) so that AMR treatment becomes very expensive, the productivity of patients and their families decreases, and it burdens patients and health insurance. Wise Consumption of Antibiotics. Considering the impact of antimicrobial resistance infections on patients, the public is advised to be wise in consuming antibiotics. This effort is to prevent the risk of AMR infection.
